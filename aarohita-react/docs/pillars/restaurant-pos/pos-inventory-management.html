Inventory Management with POS

Accurate inventory is the backbone of a profitable kitchen. When your POS is connected to inventory and recipe-level usage, each sale can decrement ingredients automatically and trigger reorder alerts when par levels are reached. This reduces spoilage and improves margins.

Key capabilities to look for

  • Recipe-level consumption: map menu items to ingredient quantities so stock reduces automatically.
  • Par-level & reorder rules: set minimum stock alerts and automated purchase orders to suppliers.
  • Batch & expiry tracking: for perishable ingredients, link batches and expiry dates to avoid waste.
  • Waste & wastage capture: record prep waste and use it to refine forecasts.

Forecasting from POS data

Even simple rolling averages from POS sales can drive weekly purchase lists. Couple POS sales data with seasonality (weekend vs weekday) to improve ordering and reduce stockouts.

Operational tips

  • Train staff to use modifiers and combos correctly — inconsistent item selection is a major source of inventory mismatch.
  • Run weekly reconciliations for high-value items and refine recipe yields periodically.
  • Use supplier lead-time buffers and safety stock for perishable, hard-to-source ingredients.
