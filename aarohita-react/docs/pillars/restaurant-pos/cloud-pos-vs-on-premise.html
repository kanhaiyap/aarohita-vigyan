Cloud POS vs On-Premise POS

Cloud POS systems provide centralized management, faster feature updates and simpler integrations (APIs, webhooks), making them a popular choice for most small and medium restaurants. On-premise POS keeps data locally and can be preferable when you need maximum control over infrastructure, reduced monthly subscriptions and specialized compliance constraints.

Key differences summarized

  • Deployment & updates: Cloud POS pushes automatic updates; on-premise requires local upgrades and testing.
  • Integration: Cloud POS generally offers easier, documented APIs for connecting delivery platforms and voice assistants like Bhojan Mitra.
  • Reliability: On-premise can be less dependent on internet, but modern cloud POS with offline-first features gives the best of both worlds.
  • Costs: Cloud POS shifts costs to predictable subscriptions; on-premise often has higher upfront costs but lower recurring fees depending on vendor.

Choosing by business model

  • Single outlet / café: Cloud POS for faster setup and minimal IT overhead.
  • Delivery-first kitchen: Cloud POS with robust marketplace integrations and offline resilience.
  • Large multi-location brand: Consider hybrid: local resiliency plus central cloud orchestration.
