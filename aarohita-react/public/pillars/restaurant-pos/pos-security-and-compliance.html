POS Security & Compliance

Protecting payment and customer data is essential. A secure POS combines technical controls, operational procedures and compliance practices. Small restaurants and large chains can follow the same basic principles scaled to their size.

Technical controls

  • Role-based access: limit sensitive actions (refunds, price changes) to authorised staff and maintain audit logs.
  • Encryption: TLS for network traffic and encrypted storage for any sensitive local caches.
  • Payment flows: prefer tokenised card terminals and avoid storing card PANs in your systems; use PCI-compliant providers.

Operational controls

  • Daily cash reconciliations and automated alerts for unusual refund patterns.
  • Regular software updates for tills and peripheral firmware.
  • Periodic staff training on social engineering and proper payment handling.

Compliance in India

For tax and regulatory compliance, ensure your POS prints GST-compliant invoices (with HSN/SAC where needed) and can export monthly sales reports for your accountant. Work with providers who understand local GST flows and can help with e-invoicing where applicable.
